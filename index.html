Momofuku Ando

March 5th 1910 - January 5th 2007

The creator of the instant ramen noodle


Brief Histroy of Momofuko Ando


  • He was born on Saturday 5 March 1910 in Hoklo Taiwan. He was raised by his grandparents following the timely passing of his parents. They owned a textile shop and educated him in all aspects of the business.
  • Ando went to Ritsumeikan University in Japan to study economics in 1933. Whilst there he also set up a textile company.
  • In 1948 Ando was convicted for tax fraud and served two years in jail. As a result, his textile company declared bankrupt and was closed.
  • Following the end of the Second World War, Japan had a country wide food shortage. Ando wanted to aid in resolving the problem. On August 25 1958, after months of trial-and-error experimentation, Ando released his precooked instant noodles. They were based on a flash-frying method which enabled them to be cooked with just hot water.
  • On release of the first instant noodle, they were considered a luxury item due to launch price being multiple times that of udon or soba noodles, the traditional available products at the time. This would later fall dramatically in price as the process was refined.
  • On September 4 1958, Nissin Foods was founded.
  • Building on his invention, Ando created the Cup noodle in 1971. This created the worldwide popularity of instant noodles.
  • In 1977 Ando was awarded the Medal of Honor with a Blue Ribbon for his services to the poor and war-torn people of Japan.
  • in 1982 Ando received the Order of the Sacred Treasure, Second Class with Gold and Silver Stars.
  • In 1983 Ando received the Medal of Honor with a Purple Ribbon.
  • In 1983 Ando established the Ando Foundation to educate young people on the benefits of healthy eating and an active lifestyle.
  • Ando was made the Director of Science and Technology in Japan in 1992.
  • In 1999, the Cup Noodles Museum was opened, which included a dedication to the man who invented them.
  • The Japanese government awarded the Order of the Rising sun, Second Class, with Gold and Silver Stars in 2002.
  • In 2005 at the age of 94, Ando completed a long-held dream to create ramen that can be eaten in space. In July of that year the dream was completed when a Japanese astronaut took the Space Ramen on a mission. The ramen would later be taken on a mission to the International Space Station in 2009.
  • On January 5 2007, at the age of 96, Momofuku Ando passed away.


Peace will come to the world when the people have enough noodles to eat. - Momofuku Ando
